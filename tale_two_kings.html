King Hemchandra

The Chanced Arrow that scarred INDIA Humayun fled to Iran after facing defeat in war against Sher Shah Suri. Delhi was under the rule Lodi dynasty now.  With the demise of Sher Shah Suri, Hemchandra became the most important general and chief minister for the new Sultan Adil Shah. He promoted Hemu to the rank of Vazir.  HEMCHANDRA- The grocer’s son!  Hemu’s origins were extremely pleb. He was born about 1501 to a poor grocer in Alwar. He used to deliver food and fruits to the state army, but later started delivering gunpowder. He was spotted for his keen eye and hard work and soon became involved in Delhi’s politics.  Hemu’s primary claim to fame is his unbroken wave of victories in around 22 battles, beginning with the defeat of Adil Shah’s enemies in Bengal, and reaching zenith at Agra and Delhi. While Adil Shah had sent Hemu to battle Sikandar Shah in Bengal, news came that Humayun had returned from his Persian exile after attaining help and support of the Iranians and attacked India , once again took over Delhi and Agra, the two most important cities in northern India. While Sultan Adil Shah was engaged at a place called Chunar, the responsibility of getting rid of the Mughals came down to Hemu. Hemu waited for an opportunity to strike, and fortune soon smiled as Humayun died by an accidental fall from the roof of his library and fractured his head. But there was still a lot left to be done. Powerful Mughal faujdars occupied strong forts all around Delhi and Agra. Hemchandra’s army covered the major parts of north india victoriously like a big mighty ocean which can’t be stopped and kept spreading area under them…it included areas of bengal , Bihar , Uttar Pradesh , Madhya Pradesh, now the last battle was to be fought between Mughals and Hemchandra himself. Bairam Khan represented the Mughals ,who soon realised they were to be defeated by a man of practical wisdom, intellect, prowess, valour. Mughals faced shameful and decisive defeated. The grocer’s son had become a Samrat!  After a long time a hindu ruler came in Delhi. Boundless loot was obtained from the defeated Mughals. Hemu took over the throne at Delhi and crowned himself Hemchandra Vikramaditya. The title Vikramaditya had been used by many an Indian conqueror since time immemorial. For the first time since the days of Prithviraj Chauhan, an Indian ruler had sat on the exalted throne. Hemchandra struck coins in his name. Various rituals were performed, and Hemchandra was coronated with great pomp. It was a series of conquests that would be seldom repeated in India. Hemchandra Vikramaditya hadsremained undefeated for over twenty-two battles in a row, Agra and Delhi had been the crowning achievement. Most importantly, he did not claim a coronation in Adil Shah’s name! But the job was not done, Humayun’s heir still lived.  A month after being coronated as the emperor, Hemu and Akbar clashed at Panipat. Hemchandra’s army fought bravely, both the wings of the Mughal army had been driven back and Hemu moved his contingent of war elephants and cavalry forward to crush their centre. Hemu was on the cusp of victory,  but it was this moment when a chanced arrow came out of nowhere and pierced the eyes of Hemchandra Vikramaditya. He collapsed unconscious.This triggered a panic in his army which broke formation and fled.The battle was lost; 5000 dead lay on the field of battle and many more were killed while fleeing. Mughals captured the elephant carrying the wounded Hemu and led them to their camp. 13 year old Akbar was asked to take the sword to a dying man ,to which he refused but was persuaded  to touch Hemu's head with his sword after which Bairam Khan executed him. .… and generations were left to wonder — what if?  What if that the arrow would have missed hemu … -That would have surely marked the end of Mughal dynasty… -Such august and venerable king would have made his dynasty not only powerful but prosperous also… -RAM MANDIR would have already been standing tall and strong in Ayodhya unquestioned and undisputed. -Aurangzeb would have never come and hindu temples would not been demolished under his rule. -Forced conversion would not have taken place…(Pir Muhammad offered to spare the life of Hemu's elderly father if he converted to Islam. When the old man refused, he was executed.) This was not just the case for one.. it was done to commoners and innocent people too. -Divisions and boundaries would not have been made on the basis of religion. -North India would have been counted amongst one of the most powerful parts of the world, where britishers would have not dared layed their eyes… -India would have never faced slavery and exploitation. -India would have continued to be the cultural and intellectual centre of the world . … but one arrow changed every thing.
